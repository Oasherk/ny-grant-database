WNY Power Proceeds Fund (WNYEDF) Grant High priority
🏢 NYPA / WNYPPAB 💰 $12,500 – $1,600,000 📅 Rolling — WNYPPAB meets ~6x/year 🔗 No match required
Description

Non-repayable cash grants funded by net earnings from the sale of unused Niagara hydropower. Disbursed through the Western NY Power Proceeds Allocation Board (WNYPPAB), which recommends awards to the NYPA Board of Trustees. Lockport is within the 30-mile eligibility radius. Startup precedent: Natrion (battery startup) received $1.3M through this program in 2025. NYPA awarded approximately $12M through this fund in 2025 alone. Eligible uses include real estate, machinery and equipment, infrastructure improvements, and facility development. Does not require the Consolidated Funding Application (CFA).

Eligibility

Businesses, nonprofits, or institutions located within 30 miles of the Niagara Power Project in Lewiston, NY. Lockport qualifies. Project must support the growth of business and lead to the creation or retention of jobs. Consistent with WNY REDC strategic priorities. Clean energy, advanced manufacturing, and carbon tech are prioritized sectors.

Application process

Contact NYPA Economic Development team first at PoweringBusiness@nypa.gov before submitting any application. Applications go directly to WNYPPAB — not via CFA. The board meets approximately 4–6 times per year; allow 4–6 weeks between submission and the next board meeting date. WNYPPAB recommends awards; NYPA Board of Trustees grants final approval.

NYSERDA C&I Carbon Challenge (Round 8) Grant Watch — opens ~May 2026
🏢 NYSERDA 💰 $500,000 – $5,000,000 📅 ~July 31, 2026 (anticipated, via CFA) 🔗 No match required
Description

Annual competitive grant providing $500K–$5M to large NY commercial/industrial energy users or to Category B solution providers selling decarbonization services to those users. Projects must include at least one of: carbon capture (CCUS), beneficial electrification, industrial process emission reduction, or low-carbon fuels. Total pool: $10M per round. Awardees receive a dedicated NYSERDA project manager. Funds are disbursed over 3 years. Round 7 closed July 31, 2025; Round 8 anticipated to open with the 2026 CFA cycle (May–June 2026).

Eligibility

Large commercial/industrial NY energy users (Category A) OR decarbonization solution providers working with those users (Category B). Project portfolio must reduce ≥25,000 mtCO₂e over project lifetime. Must include at least one required project type. Projects scored on emissions reduction, resiliency, disadvantaged community impact, and REDC strategic alignment.

Application process

Apply via the NY Consolidated Funding Application (CFA) at apps.cio.ny.gov/apps/cfa. CFA typically opens late May/early June and closes July 31 at 4:00 PM. Review the CFA Resource Guide before applying. Pre-register as a NY State vendor in Grants Gateway before the CFA opens.

NY State Consolidated Funding Application (CFA) Application Portal Pre-register now
🏢 Multiple state agencies (NYSERDA, ESD, DEC, NYPA, HCR) 💰 Aggregates 20+ programs 📅 ~June open → July 31, 2026 close at 4:00 PM 🔗 Match varies by program
Description

The CFA is not a grant — it is a single online portal that aggregates 20+ funding programs from 8+ NY state agencies. One submission simultaneously enters applicants into all qualifying programs including: C&I Carbon Challenge (Program #1), ESD Regional Capital Fund (Program #7), NYPA ReCharge NY, NYSERDA Clean Energy Fund sub-programs, DEC Climate Smart Communities, and more. The CFA opened in June 2025 for Round 15 and closed July 31, 2025. Round 16 is anticipated to open similarly in 2026.

Eligibility

Businesses, nonprofits, municipalities operating in NY State. Eligibility for each specific program within the CFA varies — the portal routes applications to the relevant agencies and programs. The CFA is the required submission pathway for C&I Carbon Challenge and ESD Regional Capital Fund applications.

Application process

Step 1 (Do now): Register as a NY State vendor at vendor.oftstate.ny.gov. Nonprofits must also prequalify in the Grants Gateway system. Step 2: When CFA opens (~May/June 2026), log in at apps.cio.ny.gov/apps/cfa. Step 3: Complete single application addressing multiple programs. Step 4: Submit by July 31 at 4:00 PM. Attend CFA workshops when announced (typically May–July).

ESD Regional Council Capital Fund Grant Watch — opens ~June 2026
🏢 Empire State Development / WNY REDC 💰 $50,000 – $3,000,000 📅 ~June–July 31, 2026 (annual CFA cycle) 🔗 No mandatory match (preferred competitively)
Description

Capital grants for projects that create or retain jobs, prevent unemployment, or increase business/economic activity in NY. Requires a 5-year job commitment. Projects are reviewed by both ESD and the WNY Regional Economic Development Council (REDC). Clean energy and sustainable development projects receive enhanced scoring. In 2025, $60M was available statewide. Funds are disbursed in arrears as reimbursement for eligible project expenditures. Grant funds subject to pro-rata recapture if property is sold within 5 years.

Eligibility

For-profit businesses, nonprofits, municipalities, IDAs, LDCs, research institutions. Must create or retain jobs (5-year contractual commitment). Projects retaining "jobs otherwise in jeopardy" explicitly qualify. Scoring preference for clean energy, green manufacturing, and projects in disadvantaged communities. MWBE utilization goal of 30% applies to awardees.

Application process

Pre-register as NY State vendor/prequalify in Grants Gateway before CFA opens. Apply via the CFA portal (apps.cio.ny.gov/apps/cfa) when it opens ~late May/June 2026. Contact the WNY ESD Regional Office before applying for pre-application consultation and REDC strategic alignment guidance. Applications reviewed in three batches; ESD Capital Fund is available on a rolling basis year-round outside the CFA cycle for qualifying projects.

Niagara Economic Development Fund (NEDF) Loan Rolling — gap financing
🏢 Niagara County Development Corporation (NCDC) 💰 Up to $250,000 (1/3 of project cost) 📅 Rolling — no deadline 🔗 N/A — loan, not a grant
Description

Revolving low-interest loan fund for Niagara County manufacturers, established in 1990. Loans capped at $250,000 or 1/3 of total project cost, whichever is less. Interest rate: prime minus 3 percentage points (floor 5%, ceiling 10%). Year 1: no principal or interest. Year 2: interest only. Flexible terms thereafter. No application or program fees — only loan closing costs. NEDF acts as a gap/subordinate lender alongside a primary commercial lender. Eligible costs: real property, machinery, and equipment.

Eligibility

Businesses located in Niagara County, NY. Manufacturing or warehouse/distribution sector. Project must result in job creation or retention (~$25K per job as benchmark). Must secure loan with fixed assets or other collateral. Must demonstrate ability to repay. Requires a primary commercial lender alongside NEDF.

Application process

Contact an NCCED project manager before submitting anything to confirm eligibility. Applications accepted on a fully rolling basis — no deadline. Submit application package including business plan, project budget, financial statements, and evidence of primary lender. NCDC board reviews and approves. The NCCED can also help identify additional incentive packages (IDA tax abatements, other county programs).

ESD Excelsior Jobs Program (Green Projects) Tax Credit Later stage — monitor
🏢 Empire State Development 💰 Up to 7.5% wages/job + 5% investment credit (10-yr benefit period) 📅 Rolling — apply via CFA or ESD regional office 🔗 No match required
Description

Fully refundable tax credits for NY for-profit businesses creating net new jobs in clean technology and green manufacturing. Green Project enhancement provides: up to 7.5% of wages per net new job (vs. 6.85% standard); up to 5% investment tax credit on qualified capital expenditures (vs. 2% standard); up to 8% R&D tax credit. Credits are earned over a 10-year benefit period after meeting job creation and investment thresholds. Credits are refundable — paid out even if the company has no current tax liability. Important: job retention alone does not qualify; only net new job creation counts.

Eligibility

For-profit companies creating net NEW jobs in NY State in eligible industries. Green project = technology primarily aimed at reducing GHG emissions or supporting clean energy (CO₂ utilization qualifies). Manufacturing minimum: 25 net new jobs. Software/R&D: 10 net new jobs. Currently not actionable at 2–3 new jobs, but becomes highly valuable as hiring scales.

Application process

Apply via the NY CFA at apps.cio.ny.gov/apps/cfa or directly through the ESD regional office. Excelsior is not subject to the July 31 CFA deadline — applications accepted year-round. ESD enters into a formal agreement with the firm that states credits available and job/investment requirements per year. Credits claimed annually after demonstrating threshold achievement.

NY Green Bank — Clean Energy Project Financing Loan / Debt Later stage — commercial scale
🏢 NY Green Bank / NYSERDA 💰 $2,000,000 – $50,000,000+ 📅 Rolling — no fixed deadline 🔗 N/A — concessionary debt
Description

NY Green Bank is a state-sponsored investment fund providing concessionary debt, equity, and credit enhancement for clean energy projects that cannot access financing through conventional lenders. Not a grant — funds must be repaid. Products include predevelopment loans, construction-to-term financing, revolvers, and credit enhancement. Minimum $2M transaction. The Community Decarbonization Fund (CDF) sub-program offers $2M–$25M to CDFIs and mission-driven lenders serving disadvantaged communities. NYGB has committed $2.5B+ in total investments since inception.

Eligibility

Developers, capital providers, energy service companies, and project owners pursuing clean energy or carbon utilization in NY State. Must demonstrate a financing gap not addressable by conventional lenders. Minimum $2M transaction. Commercial-scale projects preferred (TRL 7–9). Best suited for companies with operational revenue that can support debt underwriting.

Application process

Submit a transaction proposal via NYGB's open RFP process at greenbank.ny.gov/Investment-Opportunities/Open-Solicitations. Proposals are evaluated on an ongoing basis. An early-stage conversation with the NYGB team is recommended before formal submission. NYGB currently has no open investment solicitations (as of March 2026 — verify current status at their website).
